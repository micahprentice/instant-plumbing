The Basics

WHAT HYDRO JETTING ACTUALLY DOES

A snake clears a clog by punching a hole through it — the line drains again, but the grease, scale, and sludge lining the pipe walls are still there, narrowing the line and setting up the next backup. Hydro jetting is different. We feed a hose down the line with a specialized nozzle on the end that fires high-pressure water forward to break up the blockage and backward to scour the full interior wall of the pipe clean, 360 degrees around, for the entire length of the run.

The result isn't just an open line — it's a line restored close to its original interior diameter. That's why jetting holds up longer than snaking alone, and why it's the standard method for anything beyond a simple, isolated clog: grease buildup, root intrusion, mineral scale, sludge, and the kind of years-of-buildup blockages that keep coming back no matter how many times you snake them.

Common Questions

HYDRO JETTING FAQ

What is hydro jetting and how is it different from snaking?
A drain snake punches a hole through a clog. Hydro jetting uses a high-pressure water stream — up to 4,000 PSI — to scour the entire interior wall of the pipe clean, removing grease, scale, sludge, and root hair all the way around, not just a channel through the middle. It's a full restoration of the pipe's interior diameter, not a temporary opening.
Is hydro jetting safe for older Louisville homes with cast iron or clay pipe?
In most cases, yes. We run a camera inspection before jetting older cast iron, clay, or Orangeburg pipe to check for existing cracks or structural failure, then match pressure and nozzle type to what the line can handle. Jetting is often gentler on old pipe than repeated aggressive snaking, since it clears buildup without gouging the pipe wall.
How much does hydro jetting cost in Louisville KY?
Residential hydro jetting typically runs $300–$600 depending on line length, access, and severity of buildup. Commercial and grease-line jetting is quoted based on scope. We give a flat-rate price before any work begins — no hourly surprises.
Can hydro jetting clear tree roots from my sewer line?
Yes. Root intrusion is one of the most common reasons Louisville homeowners call us for jetting, especially in older neighborhoods with mature trees near the sewer lateral. Our root-cutting nozzles shear roots from the pipe wall and flush the debris downstream, and we can follow up with a camera inspection to confirm the line is fully clear.
Who is responsible for my sewer lateral line — me or MSD?
In most Louisville Metro cases, MSD owns and maintains the main sewer line under the street, while the homeowner is responsible for the lateral line connecting the house to that main — including any blockage, root intrusion, or collapse on the private side. Because this varies by exact location, we recommend confirming with MSD if there's ever a question of where the line becomes their responsibility. Either way, we can diagnose the issue and tell you exactly where the problem sits.
How often should sewer or drain lines be jetted?
For most homes, jetting on an as-needed basis when a clog occurs is enough. For restaurants and commercial kitchens with grease traps, we generally recommend jetting every 3–6 months to stay ahead of buildup. Properties with a known history of root intrusion often benefit from annual preventive jetting.
