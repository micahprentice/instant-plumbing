Did You Know

Shively is an independent city within Jefferson County — a working community of approximately 15,000 residents bordered by Louisville Metro on all sides. The housing stock runs predominantly post-war: brick ranch homes from the 1950s and 60s that are now 60–75 years old and showing their age in the plumbing. Galvanized steel supply lines (which corrode from the inside, reducing flow and discoloring water) are common in this era of construction, as are cast iron drain stacks that develop cracks and scale buildup over decades. Instant Plumbing works throughout Shively's 40216 zip code regularly, with experience in the specific plumbing profiles of mid-century Louisville construction.

Q: My Shively home has low water pressure — what usually causes that?

In homes built before 1970, low pressure is almost always galvanized pipe corrosion narrowing the interior of supply lines. A camera inspection confirms it. Repiping with copper or PEX restores full pressure and improves water quality.
